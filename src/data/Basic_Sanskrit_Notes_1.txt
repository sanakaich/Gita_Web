Basic Sanskrit Speaking – Explanation

In this lesson, we learn some basic Sanskrit sentences that help us in everyday conversations. These simple expressions allow us to greet people, introduce ourselves, ask questions, and speak politely in Sanskrit.

First, we learn about greetings. In Sanskrit, the most common greeting is “Namaste” (नमस्ते), which means hello or greetings. It is used when meeting someone respectfully. To wish someone in the morning, we say “Suprabhatam” (सुप्रभातम्), which means good morning. Similarly, to greet someone in the evening, we say “Subhasandhyā” (सुभसंध्या), meaning good evening.

Next, we learn how to introduce ourselves. In Sanskrit, we say “Mama Nāma ______ Asti” (मम नाम ______ अस्ति) to tell our name. This sentence means my name is ______. For example, if someone’s name is Rama, they would say “Mama Nāma Rāmaḥ Asti”, meaning my name is Rama. To say that we are students, we use the sentence “Aham Vidyārthī Asmi” (अहं विद्यार्थी अस्मि), which means I am a student.

Sometimes we may want to ask someone their name. In Sanskrit, this can be asked by saying “Bhavataḥ Nāma Kim?” (भवतः नाम किम्?), which means what is your name? This is a polite way to ask for someone’s name.

We can also talk about our studies. For example, if we want to say that we study Sanskrit, we say “Aham Saṁskṛtam Paṭhāmi” (अहं संस्कृतं पठामि), which means I study Sanskrit.

Another useful sentence is for telling where we are from. To say I am from India, we say “Aham Bhāratāt Asmi” (अहं भारतात् अस्मि). If someone is from another country, they can replace the place name. For example, I am from America would be “Aham Americāt Asmi.”

Finally, we learn some polite expressions that are useful in conversation. The word “Dhanyavādaḥ” (धन्यवादः) means thank you. When we want to apologize or say excuse me, we can say “Kṣamyatām” (क्षम्यताम्), which means sorry or excuse me. If we do not know something, we can say “Aham Na Jānāmi” (अहं न जानामि), meaning I don’t know.

In this way, by learning these simple sentences, we can start speaking Sanskrit in everyday situations. With regular practice, it becomes easier to communicate and understand the language.
